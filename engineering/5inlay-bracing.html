Top Inlay and Body Bracing

Inlayed Rosette

Before making the bracing for the top and back plates, I wanted to add a design flare to the sound hole. I decided to attempt a walnut inlay using the Carvey CNC. I milled the pieces to have zero clearance and then sanded the walnut piece until it fit tightly. I kept the walnut positive with a thin backing to keep it from cracking. The back was then sanded off after the glue-up.

Internal Bracing

Constructing the bracing was painstaking, but hugely important for the structural integrity of the finished mandolin. It not only prevents the top from collapsing under the load from the bridge, but increases the stiffness of the top and back which helps them resonate. I designed my bracing with a 15ft radius to slightly arch the top and back plates. I used scrap pine wood — in hindsight I should have used something stiffer or built my bracing slightly beefier.

Each piece was cut as a strip on the table saw, cut to length, and then glued on individually. They were each hand-carved to the profiles shown in the pictures and notched to fit together.
