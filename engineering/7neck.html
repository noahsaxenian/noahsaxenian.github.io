Neck Joint

Carving the Mortise and Tenon

The neck joint was one of the more intimidating parts of this project. It is common to use a dovetail joint for this application, but having little experience with cutting joints like these, I went with a slightly simpler mortise and tenon.

I had cut the mortise out of the neck block before gluing the body closed. I used a table saw to cut the tenon close to its final dimension and then hand sanded and filed to the final fit. I had to make many minor adjustments to get the alignment as perfect as possible.

A tenon joint is only really strong if it is really tight, and my joint was only pretty tight in the end. I decided to use dowels to reinforce it.

laser-cut template

Reinforcing with Dowels

One dowel runs in line with the neck, as shown to the right. This helps with alignment and the tight fitting holes create a better glue joint than the tenon itself.

Back panel glue-up

Finished veneers

Gluing and Reinforcing the Joint

One more incredibly stressful glue-up later, and I had the neck attached to the body! I was still concerned about the immense bending forces on the neck joint that are caused by the string tension, so I decided to reinforce it further.

Four dowels were inserted vertically, with the holes drilled small enough that they had to be pressed in with a vice. The dowels cross the edge of the mortise and tenon joint, and their glue surface is incredibly strong since they are pressed in so tightly. I believe this greatly improved the strength of the joint.
