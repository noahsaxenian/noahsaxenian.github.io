Neck and Fretboard

Fretboard Supports

Since the neck is tilted back by about three degrees, and the top plate of the mandolin has a slight arch to it, I glued two thin strips of wood to the top plate to support the fretboard. I then sanded them down to be perfectly in plane with the rest of the neck.

Fretboard supporting strips

Carbon Fiber Reinforcement

I decided it would be a good idea to reinforce the neck to improve its stiffness. Since mandolin necks are quite short, this is not strictly necessary, but I wanted to build my mandolin to last.

I decided against an adjustable truss rod and went for a fixed carbon fiber support. My friend had a scrap of carbon fiber that was the perfect size. To cut the groove for the carbon fiber strip, I carefully clamped the entire mandolin to a table saw sled. I had to make two nerve-wracking passes to get a groove the correct width. I trimmed the carbon fiber to fit using a metal bandsaw and spraying liberally with water to keep particulates out of the air.

Carbon fiber piece and slot prepared

Glued in with epoxy

Shaping the Neck

I was then ready to shape the neck. I used the bandsaw to cut the rough contour before several hours of hand shaping with files, rasps, and sandpaper. I’m really happy with how the neck turned out — it is elegant and comfortable to hold.

Back panel glue-up

Finished veneers

Inlayed Fretboard Markers

I purchased a pre-slotted fretboard for this build, but I wanted to add a touch of personality with some contrasting maple fretboard markers. I again used the CNC to precisely cut the holes and pegs, before gluing them in and sanding flush.

Finished fretboard with inlayed maple markers

Attaching the Fretboard

Then I used an angle cutting sled for the table saw to taper the fretboard to fit on the neck. Since my neck is hand carved, I would also have to sand the fretboard down flush after it on. I cut sections of fret wire and forced them into the slots on the fretboard using an arbor press. They are friction fit in, no glue required. Finally, I glued the finished fretboard to the neck.

Finished fretboard with inlayed maple markers
